WANDERLUST

"The lack of power to take joy in outdoor nature is as real a misfortune as the lack of power to take joy in books."

– President Theodore Roosevelt


As of October 2025, the United States is home to sixty-three major national parks, which combined span across about 85 million acres of land: almost four percent of the United States' total acreage!

These national parks, first established by President Thodore Roosevelt, are beloved by both Americans and toursists alike, and I am lucky enough to have visited 9 of them!

  • Acadia, ME
  • Grand Canyon, AZ
  • Bryce Canyon, UT
  • Zion, UT
  • Grand Tetons, WY
  • Yellowstone, WY
  • Everglades, FL
  • Great Smoky Mountains, TN
  • Yosemite, CA


Of these 9, I have selected my two favorites to feature on this website:

  1. Yellowstone National Park
    • Located in Wyoming
    • 2,219,789 acres
    • Founded March 1, 1872
  2. Yosemite National Park
    • Located in California
    • 747,956 acres
    • Founded October 1, 1890
